Days before Coinbase pulled off its $100 billion blockbuster stock market float last month, finance chief Alesia Haas warned of the next “crypto winter” — when prices for cryptocurrencies would dive and demand evaporate.

Haas perhaps did not know how quickly the weather would change, because winter has come for crypto. The price of bitcoin nosedived by nearly 50 per cent in just over a month, from $64,000 to $33,000, with China accelerating the descent last week by banning financial institutions from providing services involving the digital currency.
