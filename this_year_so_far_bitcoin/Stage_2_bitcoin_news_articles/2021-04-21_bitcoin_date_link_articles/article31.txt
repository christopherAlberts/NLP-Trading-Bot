Read what Ron Shevlin’s has to say about Bitcoin becoming a reserve currency in the United States on Forbes :

The rise in the price of Bitcoin, coupled with Coinbase’s IPO debut (which valued the company at ~$85 billion), has helped legitimize Bitcoin and cryptocurrency in the eyes of many doubters and skeptics. Not among banks, however. A Cornerstone Advisors survey of senior bank and credit union executives found that eight in 10 financial institutions have no interest in offering cryptocurrency investing services to their customers—and just 2% said they were “very” interested.
